🌕 Lunar Surface
Project 001 � Lunar Surface

Lunar Gateway Habitat

The Lunar Gateway Habitat at Malapert Massif envisions a sustainable, adaptive lunar settlement designed to establish a long-term human presence near the Moon's south pole. Integrating advanced construction technologies, regenerative systems, and biophilic design principles, the project aims to create a resilient and health-supportive habitat for astronauts.

Utilizing in-situ resources such as regolith, ice, and recycled mission waste, the design features myco-infused 3D-printed shells, modular carbon-fiber extensions, and self-healing systems for enhanced durability.

The settlement's linear "Spine of Life" layout connects habitation units, research domes, and greenhouse ecosystems into a cohesive lunar community � a gateway for future deep space exploration.

🔴 Martian Terrain
Project 002 � Martian Terrain

Voltaris Spire

Voltaris Spire is a visionary space architecture project designed as an Earth-based analog hub for astronaut training, research, and public engagement. Located in the Martian-like terrain of Kutch, Gujarat, the facility integrates academic, research, and experiential zones within a sustainable, modular masterplan.

Featuring R&D labs, analog astronaut training modules, observatories, and public outreach spaces, it bridges innovation with education while addressing psychological and physiological needs of space exploration.

Rooted in cosmic geometry and sustainable design, Voltaris Spire embodies adaptability, resilience, and holistic learning, preparing humanity for future extraterrestrial missions while inspiring global communities through knowledge and immersive experiences.

Project 003 � Meta Aeon

Wardencliffe Aeon

Interlinking ancient technologies with science involves studying and understanding the scientific principles and mechanisms behind ancient technological practices. It involves analyzing historical artifacts, texts, and archaeological evidence to decipher the scientific knowledge and techniques utilized by ancient civilizations.

By bridging the gap between ancient technologies and modern scientific understanding, we can gain insights into the achievements, innovations, and wisdom of our ancestors. In the realm of science, the symbols of uncertainty play a crucial role in communicating the reliability and limitations of measurements and experimental data.

They help convey the variability and uncertainty inherent in scientific observations and calculations � representing the range of possible values, the precision of measurements, and the level of confidence in reported results.

🛸 ISS Attachment
Project 004 � ISS Attachment

H.O.M.E.

H.O.M.E is an attachment module which would reduce the weight of space shuttles sent from the earth as it encompasses all the necessities along with recreation facilities in itself. This will serve as an attachment to the ISS while on halt and an attachment to the space shuttle during the mission.

This study revolves around human psychology and well-being. The module is prepared for rehabilitation of human beings both mentally and physically, and also to reduce the weights of space shuttles thereby reducing costs of space missions.

H.O.M.E. represents a holistic approach to astronaut welfare � recognizing that the psychological resilience of the crew is as mission-critical as any engineering system aboard.

🌕 Lunar Living
Project 005 � Lunar Living

Chandravaas

Chandravaas is at the forefront of a lunar living revolution, introducing an intricately planned, human-centered habitat that capitalizes on lunar materials. The internal spaces are meticulously crafted to align with human psychology and well-being principles, ushering in a new era of lunar habitation.

This innovative habitat seamlessly integrates various functional zones, including hydroponics, meeting areas, recreational spaces, and bedrooms. What sets Chandravaas apart is the incorporation of advanced technologies, meticulously woven into the fabric of each space, not only to foster well-being but also to ensure sustainability.

From cutting-edge hydroponic systems to state-of-the-art meeting spaces and bedrooms, every component is designed with a keen eye on enhancing the overall quality of life on the lunar surface. Chandravaas represents a groundbreaking fusion of lunar resource utilization, human-centric design, and sustainable technologies.

🌕 Lunar Analog
Project 006 � Lunar Analog

Lunome

The LUNOME (Lunar Analog Base) is conceived as a secure and stimulating habitat for future space explorers � its name symbolizing a chain connecting Earth to outer space, fostering a space culture for survival beyond our planet.

The project's origin stems from a vision to democratize outer space access, recognizing the toll on human well-being imposed by space industry marvels. The interdisciplinary project delved into engineering, architecture, design, psychology, and physiology, adopting an anthropocentric approach.

Construction employed advanced digital tools with inspiration from origami � resulting in an unfolding habitat that addresses sensory monotony and transportability challenges. LUNOME's aluminum frame, origami shell, solar panels, and innovative insulation systems make it a model for portable and adaptive lunar architecture.
